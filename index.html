How are planes bad for our planet?






Earth has been home to many types of life for millions of years, from humans to dogs to fish to the trees in your backyard, our planet has helped us all live by giving us all places to call home. From warm beaches to cool swamps, from dry deserts to massive rainforests, every part of life is flourishing from what our planet has provided. But in the past few hundred years, our planet has been changing. Alot of what we do everyday creates harmful gasses called carbon emissions, which over time make our planet hotter and change the habitats of all humans, plants and animals, making it harder for them to live.



What do Planes do?

While planes are really good at getting people from one place to another, they produce the largest amount of pollution per trip and are one of the biggest contributors to climate change. Planes have produced 4% of all carbon emissions globally and have only been around for about 100 years. If we keep flying planes as often and as reckless as we do, out planet will continue to heat up at an unstable rate, ruining the habitat of countless plants and animals... INCLUDING US!!

Here's the facts

  • Planes account for 2.5% of global carbon emissions
  • Planes have contributed of around 4% of our planets entire global warming
  • Our atmosphere is increasing at around 0.11°F per year
  • The average person emitted 4.7 metric tons of CO2 in 2023
  • A roundtrip flight from London to New York can emit around .67 metric tonnes of CO2 per passenger.
  • In 2023, an estimated 35.5 million flights were made by commercial airlines worldwide (97,260 flights per day)
